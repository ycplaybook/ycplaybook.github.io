    To determine Playoff Seeding when multiple teams have the same record:
    1. Rank teams according to point differential
      1. Teams with a higher point differential are given a higher seed. Note that the point differential for each game is capped at 20.
    2. If multiple teams have the same record and same point differentials, rank them according to strength of schedule
      1. For Yogi Cup purposes, strength of schedule is determined by measuring a team’s opponents’ average winning percentage. This is done by adding up the records of all the opponents a team faced and converting that summed record into a decimal*
      2. Example: if a team played 5 teams whose records were 5-0, 4-1, 3-2, 4-1, and 3-2, the team’s opponents’ summed record would be 19-6. When converted to a decimal, the result would be 0.76. The team’s strength of schedule would thus be 0.76*
    3. If multiple teams have the same record, same point differential, and same strength of schedule, rank them according to the number of points they allowed in all their games
      1. Sum the points scored by a team's opponents in all their games. That sum is their “Points Allowed” value. Whichever team has a lower “Points Allowed” value is seeded higher.

    *Suggestion: this method of determining strength of schedule is not the best. Click here for a better method of determining strength of schedule. This link also provides a formula that can be used to provide better standings / tiebreakers (it essentially provides the same information as strength of schedule, but in a more robust manner).
