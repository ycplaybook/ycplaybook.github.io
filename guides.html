To determine Playoff Seeding when multiple teams have the same record:
  1. Rank teams according to point differential
    1. Teams with a higher point differential are given a higher seed. Note that the point differential for each game is capped at 20.
  2. If multiple teams have the same record and same point differentials, rank them according to strength of schedule
    1. For Yogi Cup purposes, strength of schedule is determined by measuring a team’s opponents’ average winning percentage. This is done by adding up the records of all the opponents a team faced and converting that summed record into a decimal*
    2. Example: if a team played 5 teams whose records were 5-0, 4-1, 3-2, 4-1, and 3-2, the team’s opponents’ summed record would be 19-6. When converted to a decimal, the result would be 0.76. The team’s strength of schedule would thus be 0.76*
  3. If multiple teams have the same record, same point differential, and same strength of schedule, rank them according to the number of points they allowed in all their games
    1. Sum the points scored by a team's opponents in all their games. That sum is their “Points Allowed” value. Whichever team has a lower “Points Allowed” value is seeded higher.

*Suggestion: this method of determining strength of schedule is not the best. Click here for a better method of determining strength of schedule. This link also provides a formula that can be used to provide better standings / tiebreakers (it essentially provides the same information as strength of schedule, but in a more comprehensive and robust manner).

Statistics
Below is a brief guide to the statistical categories which scorekeepers are expected to track.
  • Points: the total number of points a player scored
    • 2-Pointer: a shot scored anywhere inside the three-point arc. This includes the paint, midrange and at the rim. For every basket that is a 2-pointer, add 2 points to the offensive team’s score.
    • 3-Pointer: a shot scored anywhere beyond the three-point arc. For every basket that is a 3-pointer, add 3 points to the offensive team’s score.
    • Free Throw: an unopposed shot scored from behind the free throw line. A free throw usually occurs after a shooting foul or after the defensive team has no fouls to give. For every free throw made, add 1 point to the score of the team that shot the ball.
  • Assist: pass that leads directly to a made basket.
  • Rebound: when a player recovers the ball after a missed shot. This statistic is the number of total rebounds a player has collected on either offense or defense
  • Steal: when a defensive player takes the ball from a player on offense, causing a turnover
  • Block: when an offensive player attempts a shot, and the defense player tips the ball, blocking their chance to score
  • Foul: any type of illegal physical contact. When a foul occurs, the referee will stop play and signal a foul. The scorekeepers are to simply record the number of fouls a team has accumulated over the course of the half

Scoresheet Tasks
  • Before every game, scorekeepers must:
    • Fill out the top of the scoresheet (write court number, circle the league, write teams playing, etc.)
    • Write the number of the team for which the scorekeeper is tracking stats (in the “Scoresheet if for Team #” area)
    • Write the names of each of the players on the team of which the scorekeeper is keeping track.
  • After every game, scorekeepers must:
    • Circle the winning team on their scoresheet (on both areas indicated)
    • Get the signatures of both team captains
  • For reference, the scoresheet used in 2020 can be found here

Notes
  • 2 scorekeepers must be present to record the statistics for a game. Each scorekeeper tracks the statistics for one of the two teams playing.
    • The captains of both teams have to sign the scoresheets of each scorekeeper
    • Apart from the actual statistics recorded, the rest of the scoresheet should be identical to the scoresheet of the paired scorekeeper
  • Runners should only take scoresheets from scorekeepers if the scoresheets have been filled out properly

Game Logistics
  • Each game will be played for 17 minutes. There will be two 7-min halves and a 3-min halftime
  • The clock will continuously run until the last 2 minutes of the second half
    • The ONLY time the clock stops before the last 2 minutes of the second half is when a timeout is called. In all other instances, including out-of-bounds, fouls, and free throws, the clock continues to run
  • The games will be played 3-on-3 with 1 optional substitute player
    • If a team does not have 3 players available to play, they must forfeit the game.
  • Each player MUST have athletic shoes and shorts/sweatpants
  • Each team is allotted a total of 2 timeouts (30 seconds each) per game, 1 per half. Timeouts do not carry over halves.
  • All fouls and out of bounds will be check-ball.
  • Every time there is a change of possession, the ball must be taken back behind the three point line (BOTH feet MUST land behind the three point line)
  • For each game, the maximum margin of victory is 20 points. A margin higher than 20 points will be recorded into the system as having a margin of 20 points.
  • A coin flip will take place at the beginning of the game. The winner may elect to take the ball first, or play defense first.

Scoring
  • Shots taken inside the 3-point line will be worth 2 points; shots take beyond will be worth 3 points
  • If a team scores, the opposing team gains possession. They must receive check from the top of the key (beyond the 3-point line)
  • Each free throw is worth 1 point
    • A player will shoot 2 free throws for every shooting foul (unless the player scores an and-1)
    • If a player is fouled while attempting a 3-pointer, he will shoot 3 free throws

Fouls
  • The foul limit per team per half is 6 (this includes both shooting and on-the-ground fouls)
    • If a team gets fouled more than 6 times in a half, they will shoot 1-and-1 free throws for on-the-ground fouls for the rest of the half
  • All fouls on the ground will result in the team gaining possession to receive check
  • If a defensive player commits a foul in the act of shooting, the player who attempted the shot will receive 2 free throws (this applies both before and after reaching the foul limit)
  • Charges, moving screens, shoves and push-offs will result in a foul and turnover
  • Under 2 minutes of the second half, if a team has not reached the foul limit, the team has one foul to give. On the second foul, the opposing team will be awarded 1-and-1 free throws
  • Flagrant fouls will warrant automatic ejection from the game
  • Technical Fouls - warning for 1st infarction, ejection for 2nd
    • 2 free throws and possession of the ball will be awarded to the opposing team for each technical
    • Swearing (cursing), arguing with refs, yelling, and dirty play will result in automatic ejections

Substitutions
  • Substitution is ONLY permitted after a dead ball: after fouls, out-of-bounds, timeouts, and between halves
  • Substitution is NOT permitted after a scored basket
  • A substitute must notify the scorekeeper when checking into the game

Overtime
  • Overtime period will be 3 minutes long. The clock will stop on every stoppage of play
  • All on-the-ground fouls will result in 1-and-1 free throws, and all shooting fouls will result in 2 free throws (unless the player scores an and-1)
  • Each team will have only one 30-second timeout to use for the overtime period, regardless of how many timeouts they had leftover from regulation
  • If overtime ends in a tie, the game ends in a draw (only applies for regular season games)

Eligibility
  • K1
    • Kishore must be a high school student (9th-12th grader).
  • K2
    • Kishore must be an undergraduate student (1st-4th year students).
    • 5th year seniors ARE NOT eligible to play.
    • Kishores who are 23 years old (and older) ARE NOT eligible to play.
